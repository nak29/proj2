Internet Search

Imagine a time without Google. A time where knowledge wasn't always at the tip of our fingertips. A short while ago Google did not exist. Before common search engines were created, there was no internet like you know it today.

Today there are over a hundred fifty million websites and billions of web pages, all interconnected and easily accessible. It's a miracle that we take for granted because often, considering the web used to look like pages of long plain text. There was no simple and effective way of finding what you needed on the internet. Search functions did not exist, you could only follow links and hope you landed on the web page you wanted.

One of the first internet search engines, Yahoo, was created by Jerry Yang and David Filo. They created it after they sifted through loads of data from obscure corners of the internet to satisfy their search for basketball information. They created a simple directory to help navigate the internet.

These search engines weren't like the search engines we know today, as pulling relevant information was still a difficulty. Search engine companies slowly stopped caring about providing good user experience and instead turned to focus on ad revenue to make a profit.
